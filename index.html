MathOnTape

Signal Flow as Compositional Architecture

Most electronic music is composed by intuition — layering sounds until something feels right. Most album art is made after the music: the record is finished, then it gets a cover. Two separate processes. The cover never quite fits.

MathOnTape is dirty analog synths, magnetic tape, feedback loops deliberately routed. Once the sonic complexity grew, intuition alone couldn't hold the system. Signal flow stepped in. And once the same engineering logic was applied to the visual identity, the two stopped being separate outputs and became one system with two surfaces.

MICR typography, archival naming, halftone-as-tape-hiss. You can't pull the design away without breaking the whole thing. Integration is the proof.
